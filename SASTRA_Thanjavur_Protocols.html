Preservation of Culture (E.Coli MG1655 Cells, for transformation)

Materials

  1. LB Broth
  2. E.Coli Cells (MG1655)
  3. Tarson Tubes
  4. Orbital Shaker
  5. Cryovials
  6. 50% Glycerol
  7. Pippetes and Tips

Procedure

  1. Prepare 20 ml of LB Broth in two 50 mL tarson tubes. Autoclave the LB Broth and allow it to return to room temperature.
  2. Leave the LB Broth undisturbed overnight to check for contamination.
  3. Separate the LB Broth into two 50 mL tarson tubes- 10mL in each.
  4. Inoculate the E.coli cells into both tubes and incubate the tubes in an orbital shaker maintained at 37 °C for 24 hours. Ensure that the lids of the tarson tubes are left slightly open to allow optimal growth of the cells.
  5. Spin the cell suspension to obtain a pellet containing the cells. Discard the supernatant and resuspend the pellet in two mL LB Broth.
  6. To four cryovials, add 500uL of the cell suspension and 500 uL of 50% glycerol.
  7. Gradually decrease the storage temperature of the cryovials from 4°C to -20°C and then finally to -80°C.

NOTE: The cells stored were subcultured 24 hours prior to the preservation for best results.

Plasmid Isolation

Materials:

  1. Transformants containing the plasmid- E.coli MG1655 with plasmid pfpv 25.1.
  2. LB broth
  3. Ampicillin
  4. TEG (25 mM Tris HCl (pH 8.0), 10mM EDTA (pH 8.0), 50mM glucose)
  5. Lysis buffer ( 0.2 N NaOH, 1% SDS in equal volumes)
  6. Neutralization buffer (29.44 g potassium acetate, 11.5 mL glacial acetic acid, DNase free water- 100mL)
  7. TE (10mM Tris HCl (pH 8.0), 1mM EDTA (pH 8.0))
  8. RNase TE (50mL TE, 100uL RNase (5mg/mL), ampicillin (100mg/mL))
  9. 80% ethanol
  10. Tarson tubes, eppendorf vials
  11. Pipettes and tips

Procedure:

  1. Prepare 20 mL of LB Broth in a tarson tube and sterilize it in an autoclave.
  2. Aliquot 1.5 mL of the broth into a 2 mL eppendorf. Leave the broth undisturbed overnight to check for contamination.
  3. Inoculate a single colony of the strain into the LB Broth containing ampicillin.
  4. Spin the cell suspension in the centrifuge at 6000 rpm for 5 minutes. Remove the supernatant.
  5. Resuspend the cell pellet in 100uL of ice cold TEG and vortex the eppendorf tubes.
  6. Add 200uL of the lysis buffer and thoroughly shake the tube by inverting it 4-6 times until a viscous solution is obtained.
  7. Add 150uL of ice cold neutralization buffer and mix the contents by inverting the tube 4-6 times.
  8. Incubate the cell suspension on ice for ten minutes.
  9. Spin the suspension at 12,000 rpm for 10 minutes at 4℃.
  10. Transfer the supernatant to a new eppendorf tube and add equal volumes of ice cold isopropanol. Mix by gently inverting the tube.
  11. Incubate the solution for 10
  12. Spin the solution at 12,000 rpm for 10 minutes at 4℃.
  13. Remove the supernatant and add 1mL of 80% ethanol. Spin the suspension at 12,000 rpm for 10 minutes at 4℃.
  14. Remove the supernatant and air dry the pellet at room temperature.
  15. Resuspend the pellet in 50uL of TE buffer.
High Efficiency Transformation Protocol by NEB - modified (NEB#29871)

Materials:

  1. NEB#C2987I
  2. LB medium
  3. Crushed Ice
  4. Eppendorf tubes
  5. Pipettes and tips

Procedure:

  1. Thaw a tube of NEB 5-alpha competent E.coli cells on ice. Gently mix the tube.
  2. Transfer 50uL of cells into an eppendorf on ice.
  3. Add 3uL of Plasmid DNA to the cell mixture. Carefully flick the tube to mix the cells and the DNA. (DO NOT VORTEX)
  4. Allow the solution to remain undisturbed on ice for 30 minutes.
  5. Heat shock at exactly 42℃ for 30 seconds.Do not mix the tube.
  6. Place the tube on ice for 5 minutes.
  7. Pipette 950 uL of room temperature LB into the mixture.
  8. Incubate the mixture for 60 minutes at 37℃. Shake the tube at 250 rpm.
  9. Spin the cell suspension at a low rpm and remove the supernatant. Resuspend the cell pellet in LB medium and plate the cell suspension on LB agar with a concentration of 35ug/mL Chloramphenicol.

Incubate the plates at 37℃ for 24 hours to obtain colonies of the transformed cells.

DNA Resuspension Protocol (For BioBrick Bba_K608011):

Materials:

  1. DNA distribution plates
  2. Distilled water
  3. Chloramphenicol
  4. Pipettes and tips

Procedure:

  1. Punch a hole in the well containing the desired biobrick, through the foil cover.
  2. Pipette 10uL of distilled water into the well.
  3. Mix the contents in the well gently, with a pipette.
  4. Leave the plate undisturbed for 5 minutes.
  5. Resuspension of the DNA is indicated by the presence of a red color due to the crescent dye.

Use the resuspended DNA to transform the competent cells using the high efficiency transformation protocol

PCR Protocol

To amplify the toehold construct for cell free reactions (miRNA 21and 20a)

  1. Add TE buffer to resuspend the gblock to your final desired concentration
  2. Vortex it briefly to suspend it
  3. It can be stored at -80℃ upto 2 years
  4. Freeze thaw the master mix before usage
  5. Dilute the gblocks to the required concentration using nuclease free TE buffer
  6. Keep all reaction components on ice and add them to a PCR tube  and transfer the reactions immediately to the thermocycler
  7. The thermocycler should be preheated to the denaturation temperature and the components must be well mixed before transferring
  8. Allow the thermocycler to run to the set cycle parameters.
  9. Run the PCR product on a 1.5% agarose gel and check the amplicon size
  10. The product can be purified by using a purification kit and sequenced to confirm the amplification of gblocks

Characterization of Biobrick Bba_K608011 pH experiment Protocol

Materials

  1. LB Broth
  2. Tarson Tubes
  3. Chloramphenicol
  4. 1M NaOH
  5. 35% HCl
  6. Transformed E.Coli, containing Biobrick Bba_K608011
  7. pH meter, fluorescence spectrophotometer
  8. Pipettes and tips

Procedure

  1. Prepare 300 mL of LB broth in distilled water.
  2. Separate the LB broth into 6 different 50 mL tarson tubes, each containing 40 mL of the prepared broth.
  3. Prepare 1N NaOH and 35% HCl. Adjust the pH of the broth in 5 of the tubes to the desired values of 5,6,7.2 ( pH of standard LB broth), 8 and 9.
  4. Keep a separate tube with 40 mL of the broth (with an unaltered pH) to use as a control.
  5. Autoclave the Tarson tubes.
  6. Add Chloramphenicol to all the Tarson tubes to obtain a final antibiotic concentration of 20ug/mL. This was done by initially creating a stock of 40mg/mL.
  7. Leave the LB Broth undisturbed overnight to check for contamination.
  8. Separate the 40 mL into 4 different Tarson tubes containing 10mL each- 3 of them to perform the experiment in triplicates and 1 tube, kept aside with LB for dilution to adjust Optical Density before fluorescence measurement.
  9. Inoculate the triplicate tubes at pH 5,6,7.2,8 and 9 with the transformed E.Coli cells containing the biobrick
  10. At this stage, each Tarson tube at pH values of 5,6,7.2,8 and 9 contain:
    • 10 mL LB broth at the particular pH
    • Chloramphenicol- conc of 20ug/mL
    • Inoculated cells
  11. Place the tubes in the orbital shaker at 37º Celsius for 48 hours until turbidity is visible.  Measure the OD (595nm) of the cultures grown under different pH values and normalize the OD to a constant value by diluting the samples with plain LB medium kept aside at that pH. (We used an OD value of 0.35, which was the OD obtained at pH 8).
  12. Measure the fluorescence intensity of the cell solutions (triplicates), keeping uninoculated LB broth as the control. Excitation at 485 nm and Emission at 520nm.
